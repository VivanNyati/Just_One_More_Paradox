What is the Just One More Paradox?

The "Just One More" paradox is a simulation of coin flips that demonstrates the absurdity of probability. A million coins are flipped 50 times, each coin starting with the same arbitrary assigned value. Each time a coin gets flipped heads, its respective value increases by 80%. Everytime it gets flipped tails, its respective value decreases by 50%. At the end of the 50 coin flips, the median and mean values of the entire distribution are measured.
